Problem

Retail staff and customers often guess whether accessories fit specific models. The process is slow, error-prone and relies on tribal knowledge. We wanted to test whether AI-assisted recognition could collapse that workflow into a single image.

Approach

I explored photo-based input as the main interaction. The prototype used a simple upload → analyse → respond loop. The aim wasn’t perfect accuracy but understanding whether the interaction felt natural and whether the system could surface useful compatibility hints. I iterated quickly with real images, refining prompts and decision paths until the flow felt smooth.

Outcome

The prototype showed that customers preferred image-based input over manual search and that AI could reduce decision friction immediately. It surfaced a clear direction for “scan-to-check” tools and a foundation for a future retail-facing product.

My role

I designed the interaction model, built the proof-of-concept, tuned prompts and logic and tested the workflow with real products and images.
