Autobarn / Tinkeru - AI Photo Fit Checker

AI-powered prototype · 2025 · Solo

An image-led prototype that lets customers snap a product and instantly see whether it fits their vehicle, built to collapse a guess-heavy retail workflow.

Problem

Retail staff and customers often guess whether accessories fit specific models. The process is slow, error-prone and relies on tribal knowledge. We wanted to test whether AI-assisted recognition could collapse that workflow into a single image.

Approach

  • Centred the interaction on photo input to replace manual search and reduce customer friction.
  • Built an upload → analyse → respond loop with rapid prompt iteration using real product images.
  • Optimised for usefulness over perfect accuracy, focusing on compatibility hints and confidence.
  • Kept the flow lean so it could sit on a retail counter or embed in an online store.

Outcome

  • Customers preferred image-based input over manual lookups, reducing decision friction immediately.
  • Proved that a “scan-to-check” flow can speed up retail conversations and reduce fitting errors.
  • Created a foundation for a fuller retail-facing product with clearer data capture.

My role

  • Interaction design and experience shaping for an image-first flow.
  • Prompt design and tuning to handle noisy real-world photos.
  • Proof-of-concept build and hands-on testing with genuine retail use cases.
