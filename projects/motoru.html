Motoru — EV Discovery Experience

Product design + lightweight engineering · 2023–2025 · WordPress prototype with custom quiz logic, Google Apps Script, Airtable

A guided EV quiz that turns lifestyle questions into three clear car recommendations.

Type

Product design + lightweight engineering

Stack

WordPress, custom quiz logic, Google Apps Script, Airtable

Timeline

2023–2025

Status

Live prototype

Problem

We started with a hypothesis: people aren’t avoiding EVs because they don’t want them, they’re stuck because choosing one feels confusing and high-risk.

From user conversations, EV events and online communities we kept hearing the same issues: too many models, unclear charging and running costs, and comparison sites that expect you to know which car you want before you even understand the basics.

The challenge was to test that hypothesis and design a way for everyday drivers to move from “this is overwhelming” to “these are a few EVs that clearly fit my life”.

Approach

I treated Motoru as a design-engineering experiment:

  • Research & validation – Talked to people at EV events and in local/online groups about how they were trying to choose an EV and where they got stuck.
  • Reframe – Noticed people froze when asked “Which car are you looking at?” but had plenty to say about their lifestyle: commute, family, trips, parking, budget. We flipped the flow to ask lifestyle questions first and let the system work out the cars.
  • Design – Mapped the journey from “curious about EVs” to “ready to test drive”. Designed a quiz that focused on needs and constraints instead of specs, plus a results view that explained why each car was recommended.
  • Build – Implemented a working prototype in WordPress. Used Google Apps Script and Airtable to handle the matching logic, store responses and route enquiries, so we could ship quickly without a heavy backend.
  • Iteration – Ran live sessions with real drivers and watched where they hesitated or dropped off. Refined question wording, order and scoring across multiple loops, and simplified the results page to three clear options with next steps.

Outcome

  • Real visitors completed the quiz and used the three-car shortlist as a starting point for their EV search.
  • We received genuine email signups and novated lease enquiries through the flow, showing intent rather than casual browsing.
  • Feedback from users and EV communities confirmed that the guided, lifestyle-first approach felt less intimidating and more trustworthy than static comparison pages.
  • The prototype proved we could deliver a believable “digital car broker” experience with a low-code stack and minimal engineering time.

My role

I led the product from idea to live prototype:

  • Research – Defined the initial hypothesis, ran informal interviews and captured insights from EV events and online communities.
  • Design – Created flows, interaction patterns, content and visual design for the quiz and results.
  • Build – Implemented the front-end in WordPress and built the matching, data capture and lead-routing logic in Google Apps Script + Airtable.
  • Collaboration – Checked architectural decisions with a backend developer but owned the user experience and experiment loop end to end.
