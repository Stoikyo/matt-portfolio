Problem

Most buyers are overwhelmed by EV choices, terminology and specs. They don’t know where to start, and typical comparison sites drown people in data instead of helping them think through decisions. The challenge was to create a simple, human way to understand the EV landscape from scratch.

Approach

I designed and built a lightweight prototype that focused on flow, not feature lists. I mapped the decision journey, created an early quiz that surfaced needs rather than specs, and iterated through dozens of live user sessions. Each cycle tightened the logic, reduced friction and clarified results. I used Google Apps Script to automate matching and route leads, stretching a low-code stack to behave like a real product.

Outcome

The prototype brought in real users, real matches and real behavioural data. It showed clear demand for a simpler EV discovery experience and that a guided flow outperformed static comparison pages. It also proved the concept could be shipped quickly with minimal engineering effort.

My role

I led the product design, UX, interaction flows, front-end build and prototype logic. I collaborated with a developer on backend architecture but owned the user-facing experience and experimentation loop end to end.
