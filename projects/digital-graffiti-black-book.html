Digital Graffiti Black Book

Creative systems prototype · 2025 · Solo

A digital take on the graffiti “black book” passed between artists — one artwork per person, shared in a chain with a reveal at the end.

Problem

I wanted to explore how creative communities share work when you remove feeds, likes and public visibility. The challenge was to design a system that felt human, playful and deliberately constrained.

Approach

  • Designed a chain-based upload model: one artwork per person, privately forwarded to the next contributor.
  • Held back visibility until the chain reached a threshold, then revealed the full collection to participants.
  • Used constraint and ritual as design tools to encourage thoughtful contributions over fast scrolling.
  • Prototyped the mechanics to test how people behaved without likes, feeds or public leaderboards.

Outcome

  • Early testers found the mechanic engaging and surprisingly personal.
  • Showed that limited, time-bound systems can spark more genuine participation than open platforms.
  • Pointed to a different direction for collaborative art tools that rewards depth over reach.

My role

  • System design, UX and interaction constraints.
  • Prototype build for the chain and reveal mechanics.
  • Iteration based on real testers to tune the ritual and pacing.
