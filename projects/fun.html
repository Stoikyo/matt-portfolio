What this page is for

This section is where I collect small projects that are mostly about curiosity, learning, and enjoying the build process.

They are intentionally fast and lightweight. The goal is to make ideas tangible quickly, not polish every edge.

How I use these projects

  • Try a new tool or coding pattern quickly
  • Build something playful with family and friends
  • Test rough ideas before deciding if they should become bigger projects

DFW

Fitness app · Local-only Android app

DFW, also known as Down For Workouts, is a local-only exercise tracking app for daily rep targets, streaks, rest days, reminders, and unlocked facts.

It is designed to work without an account, backend service, advertising, analytics, or third-party tracking.

Was Here

Digital art experiment · Ongoing

Visit washere.online

Was Here is a digital experiment inspired by graffiti blackbooks. A single artwork is passed from person to person, each contributor adding their mark before sending it on.

No profiles. No feeds. No visibility. Just participation.

Current status: Still in development. Final touches and testing are needed for email handling, then it is usable.

Why it fits this section

  • It explores creativity through constraints and handoff.
  • It prioritises contribution over attention metrics.
  • It keeps the experience lightweight and focused on making.

Tic Tac Toe

Quick experiment · Built with my daughter

I made this as a very quick project with my daughter to show her how you can build and publish something fun together.

Play the live app

How it was built

  • Started with a simple prompt in Codex to generate the game quickly.

AIce AIce Baby
