About

I’m Matt. I design and build digital products, and I’m happiest when I can take a rough idea and turn it into something you can actually click and try. Most days that means working with a team to sketch, test and ship small pieces quickly. The rest of the time I’m chasing kids, drawing, or riding one of too many bikes. I use AI as another tool in the kit – there to make things clearer and simpler for people, not more complicated.

What I do

  • Take fuzzy briefs and turn them into a simple plan and a first version.
  • Design the flow and interface, then get things far enough in code to feel real.
  • Build things like Motoru, an EV comparison site, plus other small tools and experiments.
  • Work best in teams that care about real users and are willing to keep improving the product.

Who I am

  • Easy-going, collaborative and straight with feedback.
  • Big on culture, sharing what I know and learning from others.
  • Often involved in local and community projects when I’m not at the desk.
  • Artist and bike nerd who takes the work seriously, but not myself.

Motoru — EV Discovery Experience

Product design + lightweight engineering · 2025

Guided EV discovery tool that helps everyday drivers find cars that fit their life.

Problem: Most people don’t know where to start with EVs and comparison sites overwhelm them with specs.

My role: Product, UX and front-end for the whole prototype.

Outcome: Live quiz + matching flow used by real visitors, proving demand for a simpler EV path.

EVPicker — 48-Hour EV Shortlist

Rapid prototype · 2025

Concierge-style service that delivers a personal EV shortlist within 48 hours.

Problem: People wanted quick, human help choosing an EV without wading through tools and research.

My role: Brand, site, automation and customer delivery, end to end.

Outcome: Generated paying customers within days and rich insight into real EV buying decisions.

AI Photo Fit Checker

AI-powered prototype · 2025

Take a photo of a product and instantly see if it fits your vehicle.

Problem: Staff and customers had to guess whether accessories fit specific vehicles.

My role: Interaction design and proof-of-concept build using image recognition and prompts.

Outcome: Showed that “take a photo to check fit” was both natural and effective.

Digital Graffiti Black Book

Creative systems prototype · 2025

Chain-based artwork exchange inspired by old graffiti black books.

Problem: Most creative platforms push feeds and likes instead of genuine exchange.

My role: System design and prototype build for the chain-based artwork flow.

Outcome: Early testers found the constrained, private chain surprisingly engaging.
